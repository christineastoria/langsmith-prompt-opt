# Brand Guide

Quick reference for recommending and describing our brands to customers.

---

## Electronics

### Apple
**Positioning**: Premium, ecosystem-first. Best if the customer already uses iPhone/iPad/Mac — everything integrates seamlessly. Not ideal for Windows/Android users.
**Best for**: Creators, students, professionals who want things to "just work." The premium is real but so is the resale value.
**Watch out for**: Non-upgradeable RAM/storage — customers should buy more than they think they need upfront.

### Sony
**Positioning**: Best-in-class audio and ANC. Sony headphones consistently beat competitors on noise cancellation in independent tests. The XM series is the gold standard for commuters and travelers.
**Best for**: Anyone who commutes, travels frequently, or works in a noisy environment.
**Watch out for**: The companion app is required to unlock many features — remind customers to download it.

### Samsung
**Positioning**: Value-forward premium. Samsung TVs offer exceptional picture quality at a lower price point than OLED alternatives (LG, Sony). QLED vs OLED: QLED is brighter, OLED has better blacks — for bright rooms, QLED wins.
**Best for**: Customers who watch a lot of sports or content in a bright room.

### Dell
**Positioning**: Business-grade Windows laptop. The XPS 13 is Dell's consumer premium line — excellent build quality, but the port selection is limited. A strong alternative to MacBook for Windows users.
**Best for**: Windows users who want a premium ultrabook.

## Beauty & Wellness

### Diptyque
**Positioning**: Parisian luxury candles and fragrance. Founded in 1961 on Boulevard Saint-Germain. Diptyque is the candle brand for people who know candles — recognizable to anyone who's been to a nice apartment in NYC, London, or Paris.
**Best sellers**: Baies (berries/rose — the most iconic), Feu de Bois (smoky fireside), Philosykos (fig tree), Figuier (green fig).
**Gift notes**: The 190g candle is the standard gift size. The 70g is a good "try before you commit" size. The 600g grand model is a statement piece.
**Scent guidance**:
  - Baies: Fresh, green, floral — great for bedrooms and living rooms, year-round
  - Feu de Bois: Smoky, warm, woody — perfect for winter, evenings
  - If someone likes clean/fresh: Figuier or Philosykos
  - If someone likes musky/warm: Feu de Bois or Ambre

### Rhode
**Positioning**: Gen Z/millennial skincare by Hailey Bieber. Minimalist, skin-first philosophy. The Peptide Lip Treatment is the hero product — genuinely effective with a loyal following. Products sell out quickly.
**Best for**: Anyone looking for effective, aesthetically beautiful skincare without a 10-step routine.

### Drunk Elephant
**Positioning**: Biocompatible skincare that avoids the "Suspicious 6" (essential oils, drying alcohols, silicones, chemical sunscreens, fragrances, SLS). Clinical but approachable. A Sephora bestseller for years.
**Best for**: Skin-sensitive customers, people who've had reactions to fragranced products.
**Hero product**: Protini Polypeptide Cream — their most-loved moisturizer. Suitable for all skin types.

### Glossier
**Positioning**: "Skin first, makeup second." Direct-to-consumer beauty brand known for its minimalist aesthetic and community-built product development.
**Glossier You Perfume**: Deliberately minimal — designed to smell like your skin, not a perfume. Skin-musky, warm, slightly powdery. Everyone smells slightly different in it. A good gift for someone who "doesn't wear perfume."

## Activewear

### Alo Yoga
**Positioning**: Premium activewear that goes from studio to street. Alo is the brand worn by the Pilates/yoga crowd in NYC, LA, and London. The aesthetic is elevated — think clean lines, neutral colors.
**Price point**: Premium ($80–$150 for leggings). Customers pay for fabric quality and longevity — Alo leggings typically outlast cheaper alternatives.
**Best for**: Pilates, yoga, barre, and everyday wear. The Airlift legging in particular is excellent for high-intensity workouts.
**Sizing note**: True to size for fitted styles; Accolade Sweatshirt is intentionally oversized.

## Fashion

### Skims
**Positioning**: Kim Kardashian's shapewear and everyday basics brand. Skims has expanded from shapewear into everyday clothing — their Soft Lounge and Fits Everybody lines are daily-wear staples. Inclusive sizing (XXS–4X).
**Best sellers**: Fits Everybody Bodysuit, Soft Lounge Slip Dress, Cotton Rib sets.
**Note on bodysuits**: Non-returnable for hygiene reasons — make sure customers are confident in sizing before purchasing.

### Aritzia
**Positioning**: Canadian "everyday luxury" brand. Known for quality basics and timeless silhouettes at a mid-luxury price point. The Super Puff jacket is their signature piece — instantly recognizable on the streets of any major North American city in winter.
**Super Puff notes**: Comes in multiple lengths (short, long, ultra-long) and fill weights. The long version is warmest and most popular in very cold climates. Available in extended sizing (2XS–3X).

### Arc'teryx
**Positioning**: Technical outdoor apparel made in Canada. The gold standard for alpine and hiking gear. The Beta AR jacket is their most versatile hardshell — used by professional guides and weekend hikers alike.
**Who it's for**: Anyone who spends time outdoors in unpredictable weather. The price is high but the lifetime warranty and durability justify it.
**Style note**: Arc'teryx has crossed over into streetwear — the Bird logo is recognized as a status symbol in many cities.

### Patagonia
**Positioning**: Outdoor gear with a strong environmental mission. "Don't Buy This Jacket" ad campaign in 2011. 1% for the Planet member. Fair Trade certified factories.
**Who it's for**: Outdoor enthusiasts who also care about sustainability. The Nano Puff is a universally loved travel and layering piece.

## Footwear

### Nike
**Positioning**: The world's largest athletic brand. Air Max 270 is a lifestyle sneaker — not a performance running shoe. Great for everyday wear.

### Adidas
**Positioning**: The Ultraboost is Adidas's premium running shoe that crossed into lifestyle wear. Genuinely excellent for running (podiatrists recommend it) but looks good enough to wear casually.

### Allbirds
**Positioning**: Sustainable footwear from New Zealand wool or eucalyptus fiber. The Tree Runner is their bestselling style — comfortable, breathable, and machine-washable. Popular in tech/startup circles.

### New Balance
**Positioning**: The 990 series is made in the USA and is considered a heritage running shoe. Currently having a major cultural moment — worn by everyone from "dad sneaker" fans to high-fashion crowds.

### Timberland
**Positioning**: The classic American work boot, adopted as a fashion staple. The 6-inch wheat boot is an NYC winter essential — waterproof, warm, and goes with everything from jeans to dresses.

## Home

### Dyson
**Positioning**: Engineering-forward home appliances. Dyson vacuums are genuinely the best cordless vacuums on the market — the V15 Detect with laser dust detection is the flagship. High price is justified by performance and longevity.

### YETI
**Positioning**: Premium drinkware and coolers. YETI is the brand that made insulated tumblers a status symbol. The Rambler tumbler keeps coffee hot for 6+ hours and drinks cold all day. The 5-year warranty is industry-leading.

### Instant Pot
**Positioning**: The multi-cooker that replaced 7 appliances for millions of home cooks. The Duo 7-in-1 is the most popular model — reliable, well-supported by a huge recipe community, and genuinely useful.
